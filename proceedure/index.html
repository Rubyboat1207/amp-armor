Professional Organization

For our solution, we will be using a chemical fire extinguisher. Chemicals are regulated by the EPA. We will use an approved chemical non-fluorinated aqueous expanding foam fire extinguisher. EPA regulations related to fire extinguishing agents primarily focus on environmental considerations, such as the Clean Air Act and Clean Water Act. These regulations aim to minimize the impact of firefighting chemicals on air and water quality. NFPA 10: Standard for Portable Fire Extinguishers: This standard provides comprehensive guidance on the selection, installation, maintenance, and use of portable fire extinguishers. It covers various types of fire extinguishing agents, including aqueous film-forming foam (AFFF), which is similar to non-fluorinated aqueous expanding foam. Underwriters Laboratories (UL): UL is a global safety certification company that conducts testing and certification of various products, including fire protection equipment and systems. Fire extinguishers, fire alarms, sprinklers, and other fire safety products may undergo UL testing and certification to ensure compliance with safety standards. Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA): FEMA is an agency of the United States Department of Homeland Security responsible for coordinating disaster response, recovery, and preparedness efforts. FEMA provides resources and guidance related to fire safety and emergency management. National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA): The NHTSA, a part of DOT, is responsible for setting and enforcing vehicle safety standards. This includes regulations related to crashworthiness, occupant protection, and vehicle performance. For electric vehicles, NHTSA establishes safety standards specific to EV components such as batteries, electric drivetrains, and charging systems. DOT, through agencies like NHTSA, provides educational resources and outreach initiatives to raise awareness about electric vehicle safety among consumers, vehicle manufacturers, and first responders. These efforts may include safety guidelines for charging infrastructure, information about EV-specific safety features, and training programs for emergency responders on handling electric vehicle accidents.

Problem Statement

How can we prevent or mitigate damage or loss of life from EV fires caused by thermal runaway events

Solution

In order to mitigate battery fires, we will create an expanding foam fire extinguisher that envelopes the battery, starving it of oxygen. This provides more time for people to evacuate the vehicle and first responders to properly extinguish the battery out before it destroys the vehicle. In vehicles coming from the factory, this device can be installed as a new safety feature. It can be advertised as a safety feature to prevent property damage in vehicle collisions, and other faults that would affect the battery. The device is a system of a foam extinguishing agent to coat the battery compartment in an electric vehicle. The device is a standalone unit, and has its own backup battery that will keep the safety feature active even in a blackout situation. This device will also be able to be retro-fitted to older electric vehicles that didn't have the option to have the device equipped from the factory.
