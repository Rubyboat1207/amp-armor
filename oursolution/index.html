Keeping You and Your Car Safe

Electric, hybrid, and other plug-in vehicles are becoming more popular across various states and countries as we shift from fossil fuels to green energy. As Electric Vehicles (EVs) grow in popularity, there's an increased risk of accidents that could harm people and property. Safety is our top priority. Our EV Car Battery Extinguisher is designed to provide assistance when you need it most.

Self-Activating Fire Suppression System
This device is meant to give you, your family, and first responders crucial time to get out and get away, and plan what to do. Like Bishal Malla's story, you might not even be aware of the danger you're in until it's too late. All because you weren't aware.
The extinguishing agent is a minimal-impact foaming agent that, when mixed with water, creates an expanding barrier between the battery and the car around it. This foam is stored in a strong, pressurized metal canister. Like a fire extinguisher, meant to be resilient against the years.
The activation system doesn't require any driver input, and instead relies on heat emitted by the fire. It uses a thermomechanical switch, meaning when heat is applied, the material in the switch begins to change shape and contact the other side of the circuit. It connects the circuit, and then powers a motor which uncorks the canister and lets the extinguishing agent flow out.
In the event this system fails, the cap itself is made of a metal made to melt a certain temperature to release the extinguishing agent.
How does it work?
The chemical in the fire extinguisher is an expanding foam meant to put out Class A and B fuel fires. The class simply means what an extinguisher is meant to put out. The fire extinguishers you have at home, for example, are Class ABC. Class A fires are fires on things like wood, paper, or cloth. Class B fires involve liquids, like gasoline or the electrolyte in batteries.
